Explanations of the project

THE BLUE MONOCHROME

The choice of blue is a deliberate descent. It began as the innocent color of Alice's dress—a symbol of curiosity and childhood purity. But here, it has been consumed by the dark. It is now the electric blue of a waxy leaf in a lightless jungle, the cold hue of a stone tunnel, and the monochromatic lens through which we view our own isolation. It represents the damp, silent atmosphere where sunlight struggles to exist, mirroring the internal void we often navigate.

ALICE IN CHAINS (SENTIMENTAL)

This project is deeply rooted in a sentimental bond with the music of Alice in Chains. It is more than just a soundtrack; it is the emotional architecture of the entire experience. Their music accompanied my darkest and most reflective moments, creating a bridge between the surreal world of Wonderland and the gritty reality of human struggle. It's the sound of a carousel that has rusted over but still spins.

THE VOICE OF LAYNE STALEY

The voice of the main singer is perfectly represented here because it is a voice that sounds like it is coming from the very hole we just escaped. It is visceral, raw, and hauntingly beautiful. There is no other voice that could capture the feeling of being "trapped in a box" while simultaneously soaring with such tragic grace. It is the perfect sonic representation of the "man in the box" riddle—a soul that is losing itself but still screaming to be heard.

JUSTIFICATION: THE PATH

Every element was a choice. The "clock is ticking" was the anxiety of time slipping away. The glitching names were the fragments of an identity being rewritten. Ines, Alice... they are all reflections in the same cracked mirror. The "school" video was a memory of a structured past, now replaced by the chaotic freedom of the jungle. Ultimately, this journey justifies itself: you are the architect, and these are your chains.

THANK YOU FOR EXPERIENCING THE PATH.
